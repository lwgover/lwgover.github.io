From Platform to Paranoia: How the lunatic fringe took over the GOP

Gover, Lucas. 2023.

Final Paper for Parties, Elections, and Campaigns

For decades, conservative elites were able to keep the authoritarian populists outside of the political mainstream. When this element began to creep into electoral success, Republican party elites could usually battle it back. Yet slowly, the elites have lost their power. Republican elites have sought to contain their party’s extreme tendencies by adopting a “plutocratic populism” agenda, combining the economic interests of the moneyed elite with cultural backlash. However, this alliance has been overwhelmed by a shift that is increasingly driven by polarization and revanchism. As parties have weakened, more extreme candidates have gained more control, and social media has only added fuel to the fire. The result is an apparent end to party control by responsible elites and a transformation of the GOP into a violent, conspiratorial, and anti-democratic force.
